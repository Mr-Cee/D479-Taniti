About Taniti

Taniti is a small tropical island in the Pacific with beaches, rainforest, and a mountainous interior with a small active volcano.

Island Overview

  • Less than 500 square miles with varied terrain (beaches, rainforests, mountains, and a small active volcano).
  • Population is about 20,000.
  • Tourism has grown recently; fishing and agriculture were the main industries before.

Restaurants

Taniti has 10 restaurants: five serve local fish and rice, three serve American-style meals, and two serve Pan‑Asian cuisine.

Lodging

Options range from an inexpensive hostel to one large, four-star resort. There are many family-owned hotels and a growing number of bed and breakfasts.

All lodging is strictly regulated and regularly inspected.

Entertainment

In addition to beaches, rainforest, and volcano visits, visitors can enjoy:

  • Fishing tours, snorkeling, zip-lining
  • Pubs (including a microbrewery), a dance club, and a movie theater
  • Helicopter rides, arcade, art galleries, bowling
  • A nine-hole golf course planned to open next year

Many activities are located in Merriton Landing (north side of Yellow Leaf Bay).

Sightseeing

  • Taniti City has native architecture and nearby white sandy beaches around Yellow Leaf Bay.
  • Popular options include boat or bus tours, rainforest hikes, and visits to the active volcano.
